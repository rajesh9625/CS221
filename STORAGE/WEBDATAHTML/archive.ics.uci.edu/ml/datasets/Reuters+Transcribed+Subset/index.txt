<p class="small-heading"><b>Data Set Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">Data Characteristics:<br>--------------------<br>This data was created by selecting 20 files each from the 10 largest classes <br>in the Reuters-21578 collection <br>(<a href="http://www.daviddlewis.com/resources/testcollections/reuters21578/">[Web Link]</a>). <br>The files were read out by 3 Indian speakers and an Automatic Speech <br>Recognition (ASR) system was used to generate the transcripts. More about the <br>ASR system can be found in [1]. Such a dataset will be really helpful to <br>study the effect of speech recognition noise on text mining algorithms. <br>The first work which refered to this dataset was on noisy text classification[2].<br><br>Data Format:<br>----------<br>There are 10 directories labeled by the topic name. <br>Each contains 20 files of transcriptions.<br><br>References:<br>----------<br>[1] L. R. Bahl, S. Balakrishnan-Aiyer, J. Bellegarda, M. Franz,<br>P. Gopalakrishnan, D. Nahamoo, M. Novak, M. Padmanabhan,<br>M. Picheny, and S. Roukos,<br>Performance of the IBM large vocabulary continuous speech recognition system on<br>the ARPA wall street journal task. <br>In Proc. of ICASSP ’95,<br>pages 41–44, Detroit, MI, 1995.<br>[2] S. Agarwal, S. Godbole, D. Punjani and S. Roy,<br>How Much Noise is too Much: A Study in Automatic Text Classification',<br>In Proc. of ICDM 2007</p>
