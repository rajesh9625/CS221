<br />

<p class="small-heading"><b>Data Set Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">Impedance measurements were made at the frequencies: 15.625, 31.25, 62.5, 125, 250, 500, 1000 KHz<br>Impedance measurements of freshly excised breast tissue were made at the follwoing frequencies: 15.625, 31.25, 62.5, 125, 250, 500, 1000 KHz. These measurements plotted in the (real, -imaginary) plane constitute the impedance spectrum from where the breast tissue features are computed.<br>The dataset can be used for predicting the classification of either the original 6 classes or of 4 classes by merging together the fibro-adenoma, mastopathy and glandular classes whose discrimination is not important (they cannot be accurately discriminated anyway).</p>

<br />

<p class="small-heading"><b>Attribute Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">I0	Impedivity (ohm) at zero frequency<br>PA500	phase angle at 500 KHz<br>HFS	high-frequency slope of phase angle<br>DA	impedance distance between spectral ends<br>AREA	area under spectrum<br>A/DA	area normalized by DA<br>MAX IP	maximum of the spectrum<br>DR	distance between I0 and real part of the maximum frequency point<br>P	length of the spectral curve<br>Class   car(carcinoma), fad (fibro-adenoma), mas (mastopathy), gla (glandular), con (connective), adi (adipose).
