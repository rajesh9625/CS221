<p class="small-heading"><b>Data Set Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">For each text collection, D is the number of documents, W is the<br>number of words in the vocabulary, and N is the total number of words<br>in the collection (below, NNZ is the number of nonzero counts in the<br>bag-of-words).  After tokenization and removal of stopwords, the<br>vocabulary of unique words was truncated by only keeping words that<br>occurred more than ten times.  Individual document names (i.e. a<br>identifier for each docID) are not provided for copyright reasons.<br><br>These data sets have no class labels, and for copyright reasons no<br>filenames or other document-level metadata.  These data sets are ideal<br>for clustering and topic modeling experiments.<br><br>For each text collection we provide docword.*.txt (the bag of words<br>file in sparse format) and vocab.*.txt (the vocab file).<br><br>Enron Emails:<br>orig source: www.cs.cmu.edu/~enron<br>D=39861<br>W=28102<br>N=6,400,000 (approx)<br><br>NIPS full papers:<br>orig source: books.nips.cc<br>D=1500<br>W=12419<br>N=1,900,000 (approx)<br><br>KOS blog entries:<br>orig source: dailykos.com<br>D=3430<br>W=6906<br>N=467714<br><br>NYTimes news articles:<br>orig source: ldc.upenn.edu<br>D=300000<br>W=102660<br>N=100,000,000 (approx)<br><br>PubMed abstracts:<br>orig source: www.pubmed.gov<br>D=8200000<br>W=141043<br>N=730,000,000 (approx)</p>

<br />

<p class="small-heading"><b>Attribute Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">The format of the docword.*.txt file is 3 header lines, followed by<br>NNZ triples:<br>---<br>D<br>W<br>NNZ<br>docID wordID count<br>docID wordID count<br>docID wordID count<br>docID wordID count<br>...<br>docID wordID count<br>docID wordID count<br>docID wordID count<br>---<br><br>The format of the vocab.*.txt file is line <n> contains wordID=n.</p>
