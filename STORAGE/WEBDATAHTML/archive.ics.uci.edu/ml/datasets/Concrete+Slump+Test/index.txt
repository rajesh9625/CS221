<p class="small-heading"><b>Data Set Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">The data set includes 103 data points. There are 7 input variables, and 3 output variables in the data set.<br>The initial data set included 78 data. After several years, we got 25 new data points.</p>

<br />

<p class="small-heading"><b>Attribute Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">Input variables (7)(component kg in one M^3 concrete):<br>Cement	<br>Slag	<br>Fly ash	<br>Water	<br>SP	<br>Coarse Aggr.	<br>Fine Aggr.	<br><br>Output variables (3):<br>SLUMP (cm)	<br>FLOW (cm)	<br>28-day Compressive Strength (Mpa)<br></p>

<br />

<p class="small-heading"><b>Relevant Papers:</b></p>
<p class="normal">1. Yeh, I-Cheng, "Modeling slump of concrete with fly ash and superplasticizer," Computers and Concrete, Vol.5, No.6, 559-572, 2008. <br><br>2. Yeh, I-Cheng, "Simulation of concrete slump using neural networks," Construction Materials,Vol.162, No.1, 11-18, 2009.<br><br>3. Yeh, I-Cheng, "Prediction of workability of concrete using design of experiments for mixtures," COMPUTERS AND CONCRETE, Vol.5, No.1, 1-20, 2008.<br><br>4. Yeh, I-Cheng, "Modeling slump flow of concrete using second-order regressions and artificial neural networks," Cement and Concrete Composites, Vol.29, No. 6, 474-480, 2007.<br><br>5. Yeh, I-Cheng, "Exploring concrete slump model using artificial neural networks," J. of Computing in Civil Engineering, ASCE, Vol.20, No.3, 217-221, 2006.</p>
