<p class="small-heading"><b>Data Set Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">Brief Description of the Dataset:<br>---------------------------------<br>Each of the 19 activities is performed by eight subjects (4 female, 4 male, between the ages 20 and 30) for 5 minutes.<br>Total signal duration is 5 minutes for each activity of each subject.<br>The subjects are asked to perform the activities in their own style and were not restricted on how the activities should be performed. For this reason, there are inter-subject variations in the speeds and amplitudes of some activities. <br>The activities are performed at the Bilkent University Sports Hall, in the Electrical and Electronics Engineering Building, <br>and in a flat outdoor area on campus. Sensor units are calibrated to acquire data at 25 Hz sampling frequency. The 5-min signals are divided into 5-sec segments so that 480(=60x8) signal segments are obtained for each activity.<br><br>The 19 activities are: <br>sitting (A1), <br>standing (A2), <br>lying on back and on right side (A3 and A4), <br>ascending and descending stairs (A5 and A6), <br>standing in an elevator still (A7) <br>and moving around in an elevator (A8), <br>walking in a parking lot (A9), <br>walking on a treadmill with a speed of 4 km/h (in flat and 15 deg inclined positions) (A1<br>0 and A11),<br>running on a treadmill with a speed of 8 km/h (A12), <br>exercising on a stepper (A13), <br>exercising on a cross trainer (A14), <br>cycling on an exercise bike in horizontal and vertical positions (A15 and A16),<br>rowing (A17), <br>jumping (A18), <br>and playing basketball (A19).<br><br>File structure:<br><br>19 activities (a) (in the order given above)<br> 8 subjects   (p)<br>60 segments   (s)<br> 5 units on torso (T), right arm (RA), left arm (LA), right leg (RL), left leg (LL)<br> 9 sensors on each unit (x,y,z accelerometers, x,y,z gyroscopes, x,y,z magnetometers)<br> <br>Folders a01, a02, ..., a19 contain data recorded from the 19 activities.<br><br>For each activity, the subfolders p1, p2, ..., p8 contain data from each of the 8 subjects.<br><br>In each subfolder, there are 60 text files s01, s02, ..., s60, one for each segment.<br><br>In each text file, there are 5 units x 9 sensors = 45 columns and 5 sec x 25 Hz = 125 rows.<br>Each column contains the 125 samples of data acquired from one of the sensors of one of the units over a period of 5 sec.<br>Each row contains data acquired from all of the 45 sensor axes at a particular sampling instant separated by commas.<br><br>Columns 1-45 correspond to:  <br> T_xacc,  T_yacc,  T_zacc,  T_xgyro, ...,  T_ymag,  T_zmag,<br>RA_xacc, RA_yacc, RA_zacc, RA_xgyro, ..., RA_ymag, RA_zmag,<br>LA_xacc, LA_yacc, LA_zacc, LA_xgyro, ..., LA_ymag, LA_zmag,<br>RL_xacc, RL_yacc, RL_zacc, RL_xgyro, ..., RL_ymag, RL_zmag,<br>LL_xacc, LL_yacc, LL_zacc, LL_xgyro, ..., LL_ymag, LL_zmag.<br><br>Therefore,<br>columns  1-9  correspond to the sensors in unit 1 (T), <br>columns 10-18 correspond to the sensors in unit 2 (RA), <br>columns 19-27 correspond to the sensors in unit 3 (LA), <br>columns 28-36 correspond to the sensors in unit 4 (RL), <br>columns 37-45 correspond to the sensors in unit 5 (LL). <br></p>
