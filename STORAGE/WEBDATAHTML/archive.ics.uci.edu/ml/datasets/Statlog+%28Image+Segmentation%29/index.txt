<p class="small-heading"><b>Data Set Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">The instances were drawn randomly from a database of 7 outdoor images.  The images were handsegmented to create a classification for every pixel.  <br><br>Each instance is a 3x3 region.<br></p>

<br />

<p class="small-heading"><b>Attribute Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">1.  region-centroid-col:  the column of the center pixel of the region.<br>2.  region-centroid-row:  the row of the center pixel of the region.<br>3.  region-pixel-count:  the number of pixels in a region = 9.<br>4.  short-line-density-5:  the results of a line extractoin algorithm that counts how many lines of length 5 (any orientation) with low contrast, less than or equal to 5, go through the region.<br>5.  short-line-density-2:  same as short-line-density-5 but counts lines of high contrast, greater than 5.<br>6.  vedge-mean:  measure the contrast of horizontally adjacent pixels in the region.  There are 6, the mean and standard deviation are given.  This attribute is used as a vertical edge detector.<br>7.  vegde-sd:  (see 6)<br>8.  hedge-mean:  measures the contrast of vertically adjacent pixels. Used for horizontal line detection. <br>9.  hedge-sd: (see 8).<br>10. intensity-mean:  the average over the region of (R + G + B)/3<br>11. rawred-mean: the average over the region of the R value.<br>12. rawblue-mean: the average over the region of the B value.<br>13. rawgreen-mean: the average over the region of the G value.<br>14. exred-mean: measure the excess red:  (2R - (G + B))<br>15. exblue-mean: measure the excess blue:  (2B - (G + R))<br>16. exgreen-mean: measure the excess green:  (2G - (R + B))<br>17. value-mean:  3-d nonlinear transformation of RGB. (Algorithm can be found in Foley and VanDam, Fundamentals of Interactive Computer Graphics)<br>18. saturatoin-mean:  (see 17)<br>19. hue-mean:  (see 17)<br><br>Classes: <br>1 = brickface, <br>2 = sky, <br>3 = foliage, <br>4 = cement, <br>5 = window, <br>6 = path, <br>7 = grass.<br></p>
