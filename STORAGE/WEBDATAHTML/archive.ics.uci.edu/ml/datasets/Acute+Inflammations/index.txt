<p class="small-heading"><b>Data Set Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal">The main idea of this data set is to prepare the algorithm of the expert system, which <br>will perform the presumptive diagnosis of two diseases of urinary system. It will be <br>the example of diagnosing of the acute inflammations of urinary bladder and acute <br>nephritises. For better understanding of the problem let us consider definitions of <br>both diseases given by medics. Acute inflammation of urinary bladder is characterised <br>by sudden occurrence of pains in the abdomen region and the urination in form of <br>constant urine pushing, micturition pains and sometimes lack of urine keeping. <br>Temperature of the body is rising, however most often not above 38C. The excreted <br>urine is turbid and sometimes bloody. At proper treatment, symptoms decay usually <br>within several days. However, there is inclination to returns. At persons with acute <br>inflammation of urinary bladder, we should expect that the illness will turn into <br>protracted form.<br><br>Acute nephritis of renal pelvis origin occurs considerably more often at women than at <br>men. It begins with sudden fever, which reaches, and sometimes exceeds 40C. The fever <br>is accompanied by shivers and one- or both-side lumbar pains, which are sometimes very <br>strong. Symptoms of acute inflammation of urinary bladder appear very often. Quite not <br>infrequently there are nausea and vomiting and spread pains of whole abdomen.<br><br>The data was created by a medical expert as a data set to test the expert system, which <br>will perform the presumptive diagnosis of two diseases of urinary system.  The basis for <br>rules detection was Rough Sets Theory.  Each instance represents an potential patient.<br><br>The data is in an ASCII file. Attributes are separated by TAB.<br>Each line of the data file starts with a digit which tells the temperature of patient.<br><br>  -- Attribute lines:<br>       For example, '35,9	no	no	yes	yes	yes	yes	no'<br>       Where:<br>	 '35,9'	Temperature of patient 	<br>	 'no'	Occurrence of nausea 	<br>	 'no'	Lumbar pain  	<br>	 'yes'	Urine pushing (continuous need for urination)  	<br>	 'yes'	Micturition pains  <br>	 'yes'	Burning of urethra, itch, swelling of urethra outlet 	<br>	 'yes'	decision: Inflammation of urinary bladder  <br>	 'no'	decision: Nephritis of renal pelvis origin </p>

<br />

<p class="small-heading"><b>Attribute Information:</b></p>
<p class="normal"> a1	Temperature of patient  { 35C-42C }	<br> a2	Occurrence of nausea  { yes, no }	<br> a3	Lumbar pain  { yes, no }	<br> a4	Urine pushing (continuous need for urination)  { yes, no }	<br> a5	Micturition pains  { yes, no }	<br> a6	Burning of urethra, itch, swelling of urethra outlet  { yes, no }	<br> d1	decision: Inflammation of urinary bladder  { yes, no }	<br> d2	decision: Nephritis of renal pelvis origin { yes, no }	</p>
